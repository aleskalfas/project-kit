## Summary

On macOS, `uv` provably cannot run inside Claude Code's Seatbelt box ([ADR-014](ADR-014-macos-sandbox-platform-stance.md)) — a fixed `SCDynamicStore` denial panics it, and the only escape is to exclude the `pkit`/`uv` command from the box. Until now `pkit permissions setup autonomy` only *named* that exclusion gesture for the operator to run by hand, because excluding a command widens the boundary and [ADR-008](ADR-008-confinement-allowances.md) rule 4 forbids `setup autonomy` from silently applying any widening allowance.

This record carves one narrow, defensible exception: `setup autonomy` MAY auto-apply the exclusion **for the platform-mandatory, required-only subclass** — and only when it has *verified the exclusion is still necessary*, not merely believed it. The gate is a `uv` version check against the known-*fixed* release: while no fixed release is known (the case today — the panic is present on every current `uv`) or the installed `uv` is below it, the exclusion is applied; at or above the fixed release, the box can host `uv` again and `setup autonomy` leaves it confined (and removes any exclusion it previously auto-applied). The auto-applied exclusion is loud (the existing UNCONFINED banner fires), reported by `sandbox status` / `probe` under a distinct provenance tag, applied through the real `sandbox exclude` primitive (so it stays individually reversible), and — unchanged from ADR-008 — **never written to a committed file**.

This reverses the specific alternative ADR-014 rejected ("auto-apply the exclusion in setup autonomy") and narrows ADR-008 rule 4 for this one subclass. It does **not** touch the general widening discipline (optional widenings like `gh`/`docker` stay nudge-only), the never-persisted invariant, the always-reported invariant, or ADR-004's fail-closed / believed-but-absent-boundary invariant. The record lands `status: proposed`; operator sign-off flips it to `accepted` per the [PRJ-005](../../../.pkit/decisions/project/PRJ-005-adopt-adrs.md) acceptance gate.

## Context

[ADR-014](ADR-014-macos-sandbox-platform-stance.md) established that on macOS the `pkit`/`uv` command cannot run under Seatbelt — `uv` 0.9.8 panics at a fixed, non-configurable `SCDynamicStore` mach-service denial that no `narrowing` accommodation and no env lever satisfies — so the command must be carved out of the box via an `excludedCommands` (`sandbox exclude`) *widening* allowance. ADR-014 explicitly **declined to auto-apply** that exclusion: its rejected-alternatives list records "Auto-apply the exclusion in `setup autonomy`. Rejected — `excludedCommands` widens the boundary, and ADR-008 rule 4 / ADR-004 rule 4 forbid silently applying a widening allowance. `setup autonomy` names the gesture; the operator runs it." ADR-014 decision point 4 also recorded the preferred end-state: if a fixed `uv` ships, "require `uv ≥ X`" (a version floor) supersedes the exclusion, because confinement would once again cover pkit's own runtime.

[ADR-008](ADR-008-confinement-allowances.md) is the surface ADR-014's exclusion rides. Its rule 4 governs the *widening* class: a widening allowance is the only way to carve a command out of the box, carries a per-invocation "…runs OUTSIDE the box — unconfined" banner, is **never written to a committed file**, is **never proposed by `detect`**, and is **never applied by `setup autonomy`**. Rule 4 thus bundles four guarantees onto every widening: applied-only-by-explicit-gesture, loud, never-persisted, always-reported. The organising principle behind it ([ADR-004](ADR-004-autonomy-intent-confinement.md)'s believed-but-absent-boundary trap) is that auto-applying a widening lets the operator believe the box confined everything when it did not.

[ADR-007](ADR-007-setup-command-class.md) fixes the contract `setup autonomy` is an instance of: rule 2 "owns nothing" (a setup command composes accepted primitives and introduces no new mutation engine — every effect is some primitive's effect, individually reversible by its own verb) and rule 6 "dangerous flags never ride the orchestrator" (escape hatches stay on the primitive that owns them; the orchestrator is fail-closed-only and refuses to forward them).

The forcing question for this record (its producing issue, #255) is whether the macOS *required* exclusion — the one ADR-014 proved the box cannot avoid — must really stay a manual operator gesture, or whether `setup autonomy` can stand it up automatically without reintroducing the believed-but-absent-boundary trap that rule 4 exists to prevent. The producing session ran the `critic` and `architect` reviewers; their consult established the safety conditions recorded in the Decision as load-bearing — the change is defensible only with all of them present. The record lands `status: proposed`; the operator's sign-off is the acceptance gesture that flips it to `accepted` per [PRJ-005](../../../.pkit/decisions/project/PRJ-005-adopt-adrs.md). As project-kit's own architecture-decision record, harness and platform specifics are in scope here, unlike the harness-neutral COR-028.

## Decision

**`pkit permissions setup autonomy` MAY auto-apply a sandbox exclusion — but only for the platform-mandatory, required-only subclass, and only after verifying the exclusion is still necessary. All other widenings stay on ADR-008 rule 4's manual, nudge-only path. The auto-applied exclusion remains loud, reported under a distinct provenance tag, applied through the real primitive, and never persisted to a committed file.** Each condition below is load-bearing; remove any one and the carve-out is not defensible.

1. **Necessity-verified, not believed.** Auto-apply is gated on the panic *still occurring*. The gate is a `uv` version check against the known-*fixed* release: auto-apply when **no fixed release is known** (today's state — the SystemConfiguration panic is present on every current `uv`) **or** the installed `uv` is **below** a known-fixed release; a `uv` **at or above** the fixed release → leave the command confined, do not exclude. (The first-observed-bad release, 0.9.8, is recorded informationally only; it does **not** gate auto-apply — every release until a fix ships is affected, so an above-0.9.8 still-unfixed `uv` is correctly still excluded.) A fixed `uv` self-disables the auto-apply. This directly implements ADR-014 decision point 4's version-floor supersession: the floor is the cleaner end-state, and reaching it removes the need for the exclusion. The looser platform+command-name predicate (macOS AND command is `pkit`/`uv`) MAY still drive the *nudge* path; only the *auto-apply* path requires the version-floor conjunct.

2. **Required-only.** Auto-apply touches *only* the platform-mandatory required exclusion. Optional widenings — carving out `gh`, `docker`, or any tool the box *could* host but the operator wants unconfined for convenience — stay strictly on ADR-008 rule 4's nudge-only path; the auto path never touches them. Cross-platform: the auto path **never** excludes on Linux/WSL2 (bubblewrap), where `uv` runs confined once its cache is accommodated — there is no required exclusion there, so there is nothing for the auto path to do.

3. **Loud and reported.** At apply, the existing per-invocation UNCONFINED banner fires (the ADR-008 rule 4 "…runs OUTSIDE the box — unconfined" banner). The exclusion is reported by `sandbox status` and counted by `probe` ("N commands run outside the box"), and is **correctly attributed**: it is **not** "operator-set" (no operator gesture set it), so it carries a **distinct provenance tag** (e.g. `_required:`) that names it as a pkit-required platform exclusion, not an operator's hand-added carve-out. Correct attribution is what keeps `disable` / teardown honest under ADR-008 rule 2's provenance discipline.

4. **Never persisted to a committed file.** ADR-008 rule 4's never-persisted half stands **untouched**: the exclusion lands only in the per-machine live settings, never in a committed file, and is never proposed by `detect`. What this record changes is **who triggers the write** (`setup autonomy`, as part of standing up autonomy, rather than a separate operator gesture) — **not where it lands**. The committed-file prohibition is the half of rule 4 that prevents one machine's required widening from silently lowering the floor for every checkout; it is preserved exactly. (Environment caveat: this is unconditional in a conventional adopter layout where `.claude/settings.json` is per-machine. In a repo that *tracks* that file — e.g. project-kit's own self-host — the exclusion lands in a tracked file and the operator must keep it uncommitted until the mutable sandbox state moves to an untracked file; tracked as #259.)

5. **Owns nothing.** The auto-apply shells out to the real `sandbox_exclude` primitive — the same writer an explicit `sandbox exclude` gesture uses — so the exclusion stays individually reversible by `sandbox exclude --remove` (ADR-007 rule 2's "every effect is some primitive's effect, individually reversible by its own verb"). `setup autonomy` introduces no new mutation engine and no second writer for the sandbox block. This is **not** flag-forwarding: `setup autonomy` does not forward a `--dangerously-allow-unconfined`-class escape hatch to a primitive (ADR-007 rule 6 stands); it invokes the ordinary, fail-closed exclude primitive with the required, necessity-verified argument.

6. **Self-heals.** A later `setup autonomy` run **removes** a previously auto-applied required exclusion when the version-floor check shows it is no longer required (the operator upgraded `uv` to a fixed release), and reports the removal. The distinct provenance tag (condition 3) is what lets self-heal remove *only* the pkit-required exclusion while never touching an operator's hand-added carve-out — the ADR-008 rule 2 provenance invariant applied to teardown. Convergence is the ADR-007 rule 3 stepwise-idempotent property: re-running `setup autonomy` always lands the live state that the current `uv` version warrants.

7. **No-confirm is acceptable for this subclass — and only this subclass.** ADR-008 rule 4 keeps widenings behind an explicit operator gesture because auto-applying a widening can make the operator believe the box confined a command it did not. That trap requires a *boundary to falsely believe in*. For the macOS required exclusion there is **no such boundary**: the box provably cannot host `uv` (ADR-014 evidence — the fixed `SCDynamicStore` panic), so confirming the exclusion buys nothing — the operator cannot choose to confine a command the platform refuses to confine. Demanding a confirmation here would be ceremony over a non-choice. The honest line for this subclass is therefore not confirmation but **loud + reported + required-only + never-persisted + necessity-verified** — every condition 1–6 above. Where a real choice *does* exist (optional widenings, or any platform where the box *can* host the command), the boundary is real, the trap is live, and ADR-008 rule 4's explicit-gesture requirement stands unchanged.

## Rationale

**Why this reverses ADR-014's rejected alternative without reopening the general question.** ADR-014 rejected "auto-apply the exclusion in `setup autonomy`" on the grounds that it would silently apply a widening — and at the time, "silently" was the operative word: the rejection assumed an auto-apply that was neither necessity-verified nor loud-and-reported. This record admits auto-apply only with the conditions that make it *not* silent and *not* speculative: it fires only when the panic provably still occurs (condition 1), it is loud and attributed (condition 3), and it is bounded to the one exclusion the platform mandates (condition 2). The general rejection — "auto-apply any widening because a lockfile or a platform hint suggests it" — stands fully; this is a carved exception for the necessity-verified required subclass, not a relaxation of the principle.

**Why necessity-verification is the keystone.** The whole carve-out rests on the exclusion being *forced*, not *chosen*. A platform+name predicate ("macOS AND `uv`") is true even on a future machine running a fixed `uv` where the box *could* host the command — and auto-excluding there would carve out a command the box could have confined, re-importing exactly the believed-but-absent-boundary trap. The version-floor conjunct closes that: it ties auto-apply to the *observed* condition (this `uv` panics) rather than the *believed* one (uv-on-macOS always panics). It is also ADR-014 decision point 4's version-floor supersession, arriving as live behaviour rather than a tracked follow-up: a fixed `uv` doesn't just *permit* the floor, it *triggers* self-heal (condition 6) to remove the now-unnecessary exclusion.

**Why no-confirm is honest here specifically.** ADR-004 rule 4 and the ADR-008 rule 4 discipline both protect against a believed-but-absent boundary. The protection is meaningful only where the operator could otherwise hold a *false* belief about confinement. On macOS the operator's true belief is "`uv` cannot run in the box" — auto-excluding it makes the live state *match* that true belief, while loudly reporting it. A confirmation prompt would ask the operator to authorise the only physically-available outcome; it adds friction without adding a choice or a safety property. The four-part honest line (loud + reported + required-only + never-persisted) carries the operator's-informed property that a confirmation would otherwise carry, without the false implication that an alternative exists.

**Why "owns nothing" and "never persisted" are non-negotiable even inside the exception.** The carve-out changes exactly one thing — *who* triggers the exclusion write — and nothing else. It does not invent a new writer (it shells to the existing `sandbox_exclude` primitive, ADR-007 rule 2), and it does not change *where* the write lands (per-machine live settings, never a committed file, ADR-008 rule 4's never-persisted half). Those two invariants are what stop the exception from generalising into "setup may freely manage widenings": the exclusion remains as reversible and as machine-local as an operator-typed one. The exception is surgical by construction.

### Alternatives considered

- **Keep ADR-014's stance unchanged — name the gesture, never auto-apply.** Rejected for the required subclass. On macOS every operator standing up autonomy *must* run the identical `sandbox exclude` gesture, by construction (the platform forces it); making each one type it by hand is the deep-knowledge friction `setup autonomy` exists to remove (ADR-007 context), with no safety gain, because the exclusion is forced not chosen. Retained unchanged for every *optional* widening, where the gesture genuinely encodes an operator choice.

- **Auto-apply on the platform+name predicate alone (macOS AND `uv`), no version-floor check.** Rejected — true even on a fixed `uv` where the box can host the command, so it would carve out a confinable command and re-import the believed-but-absent-boundary trap. The version-floor conjunct is what makes the exception necessity-verified rather than believed; without it the carve-out is indefensible. (The looser predicate may still drive the *nudge* path, where no auto-write occurs.)

- **Auto-apply but require an operator confirmation prompt.** Rejected for this subclass — the box provably cannot host `uv`, so the prompt authorises the only available outcome and adds ceremony over a non-choice. Loud + reported + necessity-verified is the honest line; confirmation is reserved for widenings where a real alternative (a confinable command) exists.

- **Persist the required exclusion to a committed file so machines converge automatically.** Rejected — violates ADR-008 rule 4's never-persisted half, which exists precisely so one machine's required widening cannot silently lower the floor for every checkout. The exclusion stays per-machine and necessity-re-verified on each `setup autonomy` run.

- **Generalise: let `setup autonomy` manage all widenings once it can manage this one.** Rejected — the carve-out is sound *only* because this subclass is platform-mandatory (no choice) and necessity-verifiable (version floor). Optional widenings are operator choices over real boundaries; auto-applying them is the exact trap ADR-008 rule 4 forbids. The exception does not generalise, and conditions 2 and 7 fence it.

## Implications

- **ADR-014's rejected alternative is narrowed, not its decision superseded.** ADR-014's "auto-apply the exclusion in `setup autonomy` — Rejected" entry gains a forward-pointer note to this record naming the carve-out; ADR-014's decision (exclude the command, version-floor as preferred end-state) stands unchanged, and this record *implements* its decision point 4 version-floor supersession as live self-healing behaviour. This is a narrowing of a rejected alternative, not a supersession of an accepted decision — no `superseded_by` frontmatter on ADR-014.

- **ADR-008 rule 4 is narrowed for the required subclass; its other guarantees stand.** Rule 4's "never applied by `setup autonomy`" clause is qualified: it does not apply to the necessity-verified, required-only, platform-mandatory exclusion governed by this record. Rule 4's never-persisted, always-reported, and per-invocation-banner guarantees stand untouched, as do rules 1–3 and 5–6 in full. The provenance discipline (rule 2) is *extended*, not relaxed: the auto-applied exclusion carries a distinct `_required:`-class tag so teardown and self-heal remove only it. This narrows rule 4 in place; it is not a supersession of ADR-008.

- **ADR-004 rule 4 / the fail-closed invariant is untouched.** This record widens *one already-forced excluded command*; it does not suppress prompts on an absent or unverified box, and it does not relax `failIfUnavailable` / `allowUnsandboxedCommands`. The believed-but-absent-boundary invariant is honoured — indeed it is the engine of condition 1 (auto-apply fires only when the boundary provably cannot hold for this command).

- **ADR-007's setup-command contract holds.** "Owns nothing" (rule 2) and "dangerous flags never ride the orchestrator" (rule 6) are preserved: the auto-apply shells to the existing exclude primitive with a necessity-verified argument; it is not flag-forwarding and introduces no new writer. Stepwise-idempotent convergence (rule 3) covers the self-heal removal. Teardown (`setup autonomy down`, rule 7) reverses the auto-applied exclusion and reports it.

- **Implementation is separate work, not this ADR.** The version-floor constant and check, the `_required:` provenance tag in the sandbox-block writer, the `setup autonomy` auto-apply + self-heal step, the `sandbox status` / `probe` attribution, and tests land in the implementing issue (#256). This record sanctions building them on acceptance.

- **Versioning.** Behaviour-observable surface change (`setup autonomy` gains an auto-apply step; `sandbox status` gains a provenance tag) → the implementing change bumps `.pkit/VERSION` per PRJ-002. This decision record alone does not bump.

- **Cross-platform and cross-harness.** The auto path is inert on Linux/WSL2 (bubblewrap) where no required exclusion exists, and inert on any `uv` at or above the fixed floor. The carve-out is keyed to a verified platform-mandatory necessity, so it carries to any future adapter only where that adapter declares an equivalently forced, version-gated exclusion — it does not hand future adapters a general auto-widen license.
