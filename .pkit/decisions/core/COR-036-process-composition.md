The substrate gains its one genuinely new engine capability: a process can **resolve another process's terminal outcome** — ask "has that process reached a finish, and which one?" and read the answer as an input to its own gate. **Composition** — a parent embedding an inner process via a `subprocess` state — is simply that resolution put to direct use: the parent runs an inner process, then reads its outcome to decide what to do next. The capability resolves **one** inner process at a time (never a crowd); tallying *many* inner processes is a separate, later consumer (cascade). All coupling lives in the parent; the inner process references nothing upward, so it stays reusable.

## Context

The process substrate ([COR-033](COR-033-process-substrate.md)) ships single processes — detection, guarded transitions, and (via COR-034/COR-035) blocked waits and invariants — and *names but defers* (COR-033 P5) **composition**: one process embedded in another, the inner outcome feeding the outer's gate. Every slot so far reused the existing single-subject predicate runner; composition is different — it needs the engine to do something genuinely new: **resolve one process's terminal outcome and expose it as an input to another process**. That cross-process resolution is the real foundational unit, and it has two consumers:

- **Composition** (this record) — a parent embeds *one* inner process and reads its *one* outcome. This is the resolution used directly.
- **Cascade** (a separate, deferred record) — enumerate *many* keyed inner subjects and *fold* their outcomes ("how many reached `verified`?"). This is cross-subject breadth, which COR-032 holds back; it ships when its own binding demands it, and it builds on this record's resolution as its per-subject step.

This record ships the resolution capability **with composition folded in** (composition is the capability's only direct-use surface — there is no useful "bare resolution" to ship without the embedding syntax that invokes it). Cascade is the named second consumer, deferred.

**Grounding (COR-007 recurrence).** The live binding is the trip-planner adopter's process: a per-point **verification** mini-process (`draft → verified`) embedded in its area-discovery stage, where the parent reads *one* point's `verified` outcome to proceed — a genuine single-inner resolution. The *aggregate* over an area's points ("are they **all** verified?") is the cascade consumer, deferred. Two distinct things ground this record, and they should not be conflated: the **slot** un-defers on *one demanding live binding* (the trip-planning embedding) — the same single-binding gate COR-032 used to ship `keyed`; the **design-once** decision (build the resolution as one shared capability rather than per-consumer) is what the *two consumers* — composition (live) and cascade (named, deferred) — justify. So this is not a claim of two live bindings; it is one live binding for the slot plus a named-deferred second consumer for the shared mechanism, which is exactly the COR-033 P5 pattern (name the slot, ship on the demanding binding, un-defer the second consumer when it arrives).

Composition is the slot that most stretches the substrate's "position is inferred from reality" guarantee (COR-033 P3), because a subprocess state's *position is another process's outcome*. The bulk of this record is making that composition sound — and drawing a hard line against the cross-subject aggregate that belongs to cascade, not here.

## Decision

**In plain terms:** a parent process may contain a step that *is* another process; when that inner process finishes at a named outcome, the parent reads "inner reached outcome X" — resolved live from the inner process's own state — and moves on accordingly. The engine resolves **one** inner process at a time and never reaches across a crowd of them.

A process may declare a public **interface** = `{ inputs, outcomes }`: the `inputs` it needs to start, and its `outcomes` — its named terminal states (a `terminal: true` state is an outcome). A parent embeds an inner process through a **`subprocess` state** that `runs: <capability>:<process-id>`, supplies the inner process's `inputs` on entry, and wires the inner process's `outcomes` to its own outgoing transitions ("inner reached outcome X" is the fact a parent gate reads to leave the subprocess state).

Five rules bound it:

- **The new engine capability — resolve one inner outcome, by recursive resolution.** The engine resolves an inner process's terminal outcome by running the inner process's *own* deterministic detection (a recursive resolution of the inner's position to a terminal state) and exposing the result as an input the embedding process's detection/gate can read. This is the genuinely new machinery — every prior slot reused the predicate runner; this resolves across a process boundary. It is designed **once**, here. Cascade consumes it as its per-subject step (below); no second cross-process mechanism is sanctioned.
- **One *determinate* inner process — never enumerate.** The capability resolves the outcome of **one** inner process whose subject is determinate: either the inner process is `singleton` (one journey, no id needed), or the caller supplies the one inner subject id to resolve. It resolves *that one* and never enumerates a keyed inner process's subjects — enumeration is the breadth COR-032 holds back ("the engine never enumerates subjects"), and folding across many inner subjects ("count the verified ones") is the **cascade** consumer's job (deferred), which calls this resolution once per subject it enumerates. The line between this record and cascade runs exactly here: single-resolution vs enumerate-and-fold.
- **Coupling lives in the parent; the child is parent-agnostic.** The parent names the child, supplies its inputs, and maps its outcomes; the child references nothing upward — it does not know it is embedded — so the same inner process is reusable under any parent. (The subroutine contract, applied to processes.)
- **No cycles.** A process may not embed itself, directly or transitively (A runs A; A runs B runs A). The engine rejects a cyclic embedding graph, failing closed (surfaced, like an unrecognised gate kind) — because a cyclic resolution does not terminate, and a non-terminating resolution has no definite answer. This is a new correctness boundary the flat engine never needed; it is distinct from COR-034's deferred `deadlock` (a mutually-blocking cycle of *peer* subjects — a different graph).
- **Position inferred, validator deterministic (P3/P6 hold).** A subprocess state's position *is* the inner process's terminal outcome — and that outcome is itself inferred-from-reality by the inner process's own deterministic detection. Resolving it is therefore **one more definite answer over reality, composed** — not a stored value, not an exception to P3. "Where is the subject?" reduces to "what outcome did the inner process reach?", which the inner engine answers deterministically; with cycles forbidden, the composition terminates and reduces to a definite resolution (P6). Determinism is preserved by composition of deterministic parts.

### Waiting on the inner process

While an embedded inner process has **not yet** reached a terminal outcome, the parent is parked in the `subprocess` state with no satisfiable outgoing move — it is *waiting on the subprocess*. This is the `awaiting-subprocess-outcome` blocked reason that COR-034 named and forwarded to "the slot where the engine gains the right to read across the process boundary" — which is this slot. It un-defers **here, for the single-inner case**, as an additive blocked reason reusing COR-034's existing derived-overlay model: it is **auto-clearing** (like `awaiting-condition`) — the "condition" is "the inner process reached outcome X", re-evaluated live by the resolution above; when it holds, the wait clears and the parent moves. It is not a new state and not a new wait mechanism. (The *aggregate* wait — "waiting until **all** of many inner subjects finish" — is cross-subject and stays with cascade. And COR-034 forwarded `awaiting-subprocess-outcome` as a cross-subject reason; this record *refines* that — the single-inner case reads exactly one inner subject, so it is single-subject-establishable and lands here, while the many-inner aggregate remains the cross-subject remainder for cascade. A refinement of COR-034's framing, not a contradiction: COR-034 forwarded the reason to the slot that gains cross-boundary reading, which is precisely here.)

Outcome→transition wiring completeness is the author's responsibility, not the engine's (as with state reachability today): the engine does not require that every inner outcome be wired to an outgoing transition. So if the inner finishes at an outcome the parent did not wire, the parent is *correctly* still `awaiting-subprocess-outcome` — it has no move its author gave it — rather than mis-reporting; the wait reflects an incomplete parent definition, which the binding owns.

### Scope — nest/call only

This ships the **nest/call** timing: the parent embeds an inner process and reads its outcome. The **overflow / hand-off** timing — a terminal state spawning or unblocking a *concurrent* sibling process — is altitude-2 orchestration (COR-033's named `orchestration` slot) and is **deferred**, along with the aggregate enumerate-and-fold (cascade) and anything else the live binding does not demand.

## Rationale

This is name-broad / ship-narrow ([COR-016](COR-016-scripted-scenario-storyboards.md)) applied as COR-033 P5 intended: un-defer a named slot when a real binding needs it ([COR-007](COR-007-pattern-extraction.md)'s recurrence test) — mirroring COR-032 (`keyed`), [COR-034](COR-034-human-pause-gate.md) (blocked), and [COR-035](COR-035-process-invariants.md) (invariants). Unlike those three, composition is *not* "reuse the predicate runner": it adds cross-process outcome resolution. That is real new machinery — which is exactly why it must be **designed once**, as its own capability, with the two consumers (composition here, cascade later) building on it rather than each inventing their own way across the process boundary.

**Why composition is folded into the resolution capability, not a third record.** The cross-process resolution is the new decision; the single-vs-aggregate split (this record vs cascade) is the other real decision — and the COR-032 enumeration line runs exactly between them. But "resolution capability" and "composition" are not two decisions: composition (the `interface`/`subprocess` embedding) is the *only direct-use surface* of the resolution. A "bare resolution with no embedding syntax" would be an inert engine capability with nothing to invoke it — the COR-007 inert-slot failure. So composition folds in; cascade (the genuinely-different enumerate-and-fold) is the separate record.

**Why one determinate inner, not an aggregate (the cascade boundary).** Resolving *one* inner outcome is single-resolution — the engine asks one inner engine "what outcome?" and gets a definite answer, never enumerating. Aggregating across *many* keyed inner subjects requires enumerating them — the breadth COR-032 defers. Folding the aggregate in here would smuggle cross-subject enumeration into a single-resolution slot and build the very second mechanism the design-once is meant to avoid. Keeping the aggregate in cascade — *consuming this resolution as its per-subject step* — is what makes design-once honest: the resolution is shared and minimal; cascade's enumerate-and-fold is its own genuinely-new part, designed against its real shape when it lands.

**Why forbid cycles.** Recursion across the process boundary is the first place a process can reference another by address, so it is the first place a cycle is expressible. A cyclic resolution never terminates, and the "definite answer over reality, composed" guarantee (P3/P6) is then false. A static acyclicity check, fail-closed on a cycle, is the minimal guard that keeps the determinism claim true.

**Why `awaiting-subprocess-outcome` lands here, single-inner only.** COR-034 forwarded it to "the slot where the engine gains the right to read across the process boundary," deferring it because the machinery did not exist. This record supplies that machinery. A parent waiting on *one* inner process's outcome is single-inner — it reuses COR-034's auto-clearing overlay with the resolution as its condition. The *many-inner* aggregate wait is cross-subject and stays with cascade. This resolves the cross-record gap cleanly and additively.

**Why nest/call now, overflow/hand-off later.** The live binding needs nest/call (a parent waiting on an inner outcome). Concurrent spawn (a terminal unblocking a peer process) is the orchestration altitude — a larger concurrency model the recurrence has not reached; shipping it now would be speculative generality. It un-defers when a binding needs concurrent process interaction.

### Alternatives considered

- **Ship the aggregate (enumerate-and-fold across many inner subjects) here.** Rejected — that is cross-subject breadth (COR-032 defers it); it is the cascade consumer, which builds on this record's resolution. Folding it in would break single-resolution and build a rival cross-process mechanism.
- **Three records (a bare resolution primitive, separate from composition).** Rejected — composition is the resolution's only direct-use surface; a bare primitive with no embedding syntax is an inert capability. Composition folds in; cascade (the real second consumer) is separate.
- **Couple the child to its parent (child declares who embeds it).** Rejected — destroys reuse; the child could not be lifted to a second parent. Coupling-in-parent keeps the inner process free-standing.
- **Model the parent's wait as a new "pending position" rather than a blocked reason.** Rejected — that re-opens COR-034's rejected definition-state alternative; reusing the auto-clearing `awaiting-subprocess-outcome` overlay is consistent and adds no new wait mechanism.
- **Ship overflow/hand-off (concurrent spawn) with nest/call.** Rejected — that is the orchestration altitude, unreached by the recurrence; over-ships past the binding's need. Deferred.
- **Model a subprocess state's position as stored state rather than the resolved inner outcome.** Rejected — storing it breaks P3's inferred-from-reality guarantee and needs sync; resolving it live keeps it a definite answer over reality, composed.

## Implications

- **Shape contract.** A process gains an optional `interface` (`{ inputs, outcomes }`) and may declare a `subprocess` state (`runs: <capability>:<process-id>`, input supply, outcome→transition wiring); the `blocked_on` enum widens (additively) by `awaiting-subprocess-outcome` for the single-inner wait. Additive — existing processes declare none and are unaffected — so no migration is owed ([COR-010](COR-010-resource-lifecycle.md)). The exact field layout lives in the process-area shape reference, not here.
- **Engine — cross-process resolution + the acyclicity guard.** The engine gains the recursive resolution of one determinate inner process's terminal outcome (via the inner's own deterministic detection), exposed as an input to the embedding process's detection/gate, plus a fail-closed acyclicity guard on the embedding graph. Single-resolution — one inner subject per resolution; it never enumerates inner subjects (that is cascade's deferred breadth). Position stays inferred; the validator stays deterministic (composition of deterministic, acyclic parts).
- **Acceptance-gated implementation.** This record decides the rule; adding `interface`/`subprocess` to the shape contract, standing up the recursive resolution + acyclicity guard, and widening `blocked_on` are downstream tasks that may not proceed until this record is accepted.
- **Grounding binding carries its own instance.** The adopter's trip-planning process authors its embedding once the slot ships (the per-point `draft→verified` verification process embedded in area discovery) — the engine slot and a real binding land together, so the slot is never inert.
- **Cascade consumes this resolution.** The cascade slot (deferred) builds the enumerate-and-fold aggregate — and the many-inner aggregate wait — *on* this record's single-inner resolution, not a new mechanism. This record's resolution is the foundation cascade stands on; sequencing the resolution before cascade is deliberate.
- **Adjacent capabilities remain deferred**, each its own future decision when a binding needs it: the **overflow / hand-off** (concurrent spawn) timing and the broader **orchestration** altitude; the **enumerate-and-fold aggregate** and the **many-inner aggregate wait** (the cascade consumer).
- Relationship to records: implements the composition slot named in COR-033 (P5/P6); applies name-broad / ship-narrow (COR-016) and extract-on-recurrence (COR-007); preserves COR-032's single-subject engine (one inner resolution, no enumeration); upholds COR-033 P3's inferred-position guarantee (the inner outcome is a composed definite answer, cycles forbidden); resolves COR-034's `awaiting-subprocess-outcome` forwarding for the single-inner case (additive enum widening, auto-clearing overlay); does not amend COR-033 — un-deferring a named extension point is fulfilment, not revision (mirrors COR-032/034/035).
