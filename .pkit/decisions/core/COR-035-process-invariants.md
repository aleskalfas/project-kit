A process can now declare **invariants** — conditions that must hold *no matter where* a subject sits in the process. Where detection answers "where is this subject?" and a gate answers "may it move from here to there?", an invariant answers a third, position-independent question: "is something that must *always* be true, true?" The engine evaluates each invariant as a read-only predicate over the subject's reality and reports any violation; it does not interpret what the invariant means. This un-defers one of the named extension points the substrate reserved, scoped to the minimum two bindings actually need.

## Context

The process substrate ([COR-033](COR-033-process-substrate.md)) ships detection (where a subject is) and guarded transitions (whether a move is allowed from a given position), and *names but defers* (COR-033 P5) a richer set of capabilities — among them **invariants**: position-independent always-checks. This record un-defers that slot. (Un-deferring it stands up a new engine `validate` operation — the engine ships `status` / `can-move` / `move` today; there is no `validate` yet. See Implications.)

Two bindings need it. pm (the first binding) has structural rules an issue must satisfy regardless of its lifecycle position. The trip-planner adopter's process (the second binding) names three concrete, genuinely position-independent rules: **evidence-backed facts** (every factual claim resolves to an evidence record — uncited claims are invalid anywhere, not just at one transition), **scope-containment** (discovery stays inside the declared corridor and out of excluded regions), and **derive-don't-store** (computed views like coverage and the day-plan are never persisted alongside the atoms they derive from). None of these is a "may I move here?" gate or a "where am I?" detector — each must hold at *every* position. Two bindings demanding the same shape meets COR-033 P5's gate (COR-007's recurrence test); this record ships the slot — minimally.

## Decision

**In plain terms:** a process may declare always-true checks the engine runs independent of the subject's position, reporting any that fail — without interpreting what they mean.

A process may declare **invariants**. Each invariant is an `{ id, check, why }`: a stable `id`, a `check` predicate, and a `why` — the explanatory prose surfaced when the check fails. Each invariant holds **process-wide** (across every state); per-state scoping is deferred (below). The engine evaluates invariants through the **same predicate runner that already backs detection and gates** — the single-subject runner, threaded with the subject identifier.

- **Position-independent.** Unlike a gate (bound to one `from→to` move, evaluated at move time) or detection (which *computes* position), an invariant is checked irrespective of where the subject is — a property of *being* anywhere in the process, not of *moving*. It is the substrate's answer to "this must hold everywhere," which neither gates nor detection express.
- **Read-only, content-free, single-subject.** The engine *runs* each `check` and *reports* its result (with the `why` on failure); it does not interpret what the invariant means or act on a violation beyond reporting — the binding owns the meaning. An invariant's `check` reads only the one subject's reality it is evaluated for; it never reads across subjects (COR-032's engine-never-enumerates line holds — which is why invariants is a single-subject slot, distinct from the deferred cross-subject capabilities).
- **Report-only enforcement, surfaced on every status read.** A violation is **surfaced in the status view** — the operation an agent reads on every "where am I" — and reported by the dedicated `validate` operation. Status surfacing is the load-bearing half: because a violation shows on every status read, a binding's wrapper sees it and declines downstream (e.g. trip-planning refusing to treat an uncited claim as verified); `validate` is the explicit audit verb on top. This slot ships exactly that — surface + report. It does **not** block moves, fail transitions, or remediate; those richer enforcement couplings are not shipped (below).
- **Determinism preserved.** An invariant is a predicate over reality, exactly like a detection predicate — so position stays inferred and the engine stays a deterministic validator (COR-033 P3): "does this invariant hold for this subject" is a definite answer.

## Rationale

This is name-broad / ship-narrow ([COR-016](COR-016-scripted-scenario-storyboards.md)) applied as COR-033 P5 intended: un-defer a named slot when real bindings need it ([COR-007](COR-007-pattern-extraction.md)'s recurrence test) — mirroring how [COR-032](COR-032-keyed-process-subjects.md) shipped `keyed` and [COR-034](COR-034-human-pause-gate.md) shipped the blocked slot. The evaluation machinery already exists — the predicate runner that backs detection and gates evaluates an invariant unchanged, no new position concept — so the new surface is a declaration (`{id, check, why}`) plus the `validate` operation that runs the set and reports failures, with violations surfaced on the status view.

**Process-wide, not subset-scoped** — and that is the minimal slice. All five grounding instances (pm's structural rules; trip-planning's evidence-backed / scope-containment / derive-don't-store) hold at *every* position; none needs a per-state subset. Per-state scoping has exactly one prospective consumer — composition's deferred *open region* (a state "bounded only by invariants + an exit gate", COR-033 P6) — and that consumer needs invariants in a **boundary-enforcing** posture, not report-only. So subset-scoping un-defers *with* the open-region slot that actually needs it (and needs the enforcement coupling too); shipping the scope axis now, report-only, would be an axis no current binding exercises. The declaration is forward-compatible — adding a scope field later is additive.

Scoping enforcement to **report-only** is the other half of the ship-narrow line. Both bindings need to *know* an invariant is violated (and a binding wrapper, reading the violation off status, declines downstream — the engine itself stays content-free, the same way pm keeps its cascade in its wrappers per COR-032); neither yet needs the engine to *block every move* or remediate. A binding that wants a hard always-gate today can express it as a gate predicate — so report-only is not a crippled gate, it is the additive position-independent *reporting* layer the gate model does not provide. Coupling an always-check to move-blocking changes the gate model, which no binding has asked the substrate to do; it un-defers if one genuinely needs it.

### Alternatives considered

- **Couple invariants to move-blocking now (a violated invariant refuses all transitions).** Rejected for this slice — it is a larger change to the gate model than the demanding bindings need, and over-ships past the recurrence (the COR-007 failure). A binding that wants a hard always-gate can express it as a gate predicate today; the position-independent *reporting* check is the new thing actually demanded. Stronger enforcement is its own future decision.
- **Ship per-state (`applies-to` subset) scoping now.** Rejected — no grounding binding needs a subset (all five hold process-wide); its one prospective consumer is composition's deferred open region, which needs invariants *boundary-enforcing*, not report-only. Shipping the scope axis now would be an unused axis ahead of its consumer; it un-defers additively with the open-region slot. (Process-wide is the truly minimal slice.)
- **Ship invariant severity levels / auto-remediation.** Rejected — neither binding demands graded severities or engine-driven repair; speculative. Defer until a binding needs them.
- **Let each binding hand-roll its own always-checks (capability-local, off the substrate).** Rejected — the check rides the substrate's *own* predicate runner and its *own* `validate`/status surface; both are core. Pushing invariants capability-local would have every binding re-invent "run a position-independent check and surface it," off the shared validate path a tool already reads.
- **Allow an invariant to read across subjects** (e.g. "no two subjects hold the same lock"). Rejected — that is the cross-subject breadth COR-032 defers; an invariant is single-subject. A cross-subject always-check ships where the engine first gains the right to read across subjects.

## Implications

- **Shape contract.** A process gains an optional `invariants` declaration — a list of `{ id, check, why }`, each holding process-wide. The `check` is a predicate of the same shape detection and gates already use; `why` reuses the explanatory-prose convention detection/gates/blocked already carry. Additive — existing processes declare none and are unaffected — so no migration is owed ([COR-010](COR-010-resource-lifecycle.md)). The exact field layout lives in the process-area shape reference, not here.
- **Engine — a new `validate` operation.** The engine ships `status` / `can-move` / `move` today and has no `validate`; this slot **stands `validate` up** (a new engine operation + its CLI surface) to run the subject's invariants through the existing predicate runner and report violations. Violations are also **surfaced on the status view** (the non-inert half — an agent sees them on every read). Read-only: evaluating invariants writes nothing and does not affect position or move-legality. Position stays inferred; the validator stays deterministic; no cross-subject read.
- **Acceptance-gated implementation.** This record decides the rule; standing up `validate`, adding the `invariants` declaration to the shape contract, and the status surfacing are downstream tasks that may not proceed until this record is accepted. On acceptance, the process-area reference flips its `invariants` / `validate` rows from `(deferred)` to core, and the open-region note gains the caveat below.
- **Grounding bindings carry their own instances.** pm declares its structural invariants and the adopter's trip-planning process declares its three (evidence-backed / scope-containment / derive-don't-store) once the slot ships — the engine slot and a real binding land together, so the slot is never inert.
- **Composition's open region needs more than this slot.** This slot serves the *declaration* half of the open-region prerequisite (COR-033 P6 — a state "bounded only by invariants + an exit gate"): the open region will reference these same invariants. But an open region needs them *boundary-enforcing* (constraining legal movement), which is the deferred move-blocking coupling below, **plus** per-state scoping. So "invariants is a prerequisite for composition #176" is true of the declaration only — the open region also needs the deferred enforcement + scoping, which land together when it ships.
- **Adjacent capabilities remain deferred**, each its own future decision when a binding needs it: per-state (`applies-to`) scoping and boundary-enforcement (move-blocking on violation) — both consumed by composition's open region; invariant severity / auto-remediation; and cross-subject always-checks (which require the breadth COR-032 holds back).
- Relationship to records: implements the invariants slot named in COR-033 (P5); applies name-broad / ship-narrow (COR-016) and extract-on-recurrence (COR-007); does not amend COR-033 — un-deferring a named extension point is fulfilment, not revision (mirrors COR-032 and COR-034).
